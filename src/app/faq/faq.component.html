Car insurance policy is mandatory under the Motor Vehicle Act. The policy ensures that the car owner is protected from financial losses, caused by damage or theft to the vehicle and third party liability.
It is a type of two and four wheeler insurance policy that covers your legal liability in case of any bodily or property damage caused to a third party by your vehicle. According to the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, it is mandatory for every vehicle owner to have third-party insurance.
In addition to third-party liability, a comprehensive insurance plan protects your vehicle against risks like theft, vandalism, and fire. It also covers you if your car or bike suffers any loss or damage due to natural or man-made disasters such as floods, cyclones, earthquakes, riots, and so on.
Your claim can be rejected under the following circumstances: If it is proven beyond a reasonable doubt that you were driving recklessly or under the influence of alcohol or drugs If you have exhausted the policy’s sum assured for a given period If your claim is outside the policy inclusions
Your insurance policy follows the vehicle, not the driver. If your vehicle is being driven by someone else with your permission, the insurer will still be liable to pay for damages up to the sum assured.
Once you have intimated the insurer, you will be provided with a claim number. On submitting the required documents, your claim will be processed within a short period of time in accordance with the policy’s terms and conditions.
